The base structure can be decomposed into four main sectors:

1) Front sector: This is the responsible of the stress redistribution. The stresses use this path in order to create an equilibrating moment. The middle part, situated between the front chain and the application point of the external force, is empty: in fact, the volume fraction constrained minimization gets to this result, where the stresses have to be collocated in an intermediate location. Moreover, this sector links the two series of points where the homogeneous Boundary Conditions are applied. The leading edge is not charged, being a free boundary surface: a large portion of this part could be deleted using a smaller mesh and an inferior lter radius.

2) Central chain: This sector is the transmitter of forces between the application point of the external force and the rear chain (discussed later), as shown in the macroscopic stress distribution. Its directly linked to all the other parts of the optimized structure.

3) Inferior chain: Its the main link between the central chain and the inferior BCs. In the base structure, its also directly linked to the front part: this is due to the fact that it starts exactly at the left limit of the inferior boundary points interested by the application of homogeneous BCs.

4) Rear chain: This is the only part not interested in any redistribution of stresses, as shown in Figure 7 and 8. Its main role is transmitting the commanded displacement at the trailing edge, thanks to an input force provided by the central chain. This sector is very penalizing from the viewpoint of the volume fraction, given that it doesnt have to carry any stress: as mentioned for the front chain, a more dened result, relatively to the topology of this sector, may be obtained by adopting a smaller mesh and reducing the lter radius.

The optimized structure in deformed conguration is illustrated in Figure 5. The reader can observe the logic at the base of the mechanism. By introducing a single input force at the node in green, the effort is transmitted to the rear chain through the central sector. Contemporaneously, the inferior chain and the front sector provide the central chain with a connection to the nodes in blue (where homogeneous Dirichlet Boundary Conditions are applied). Finally, the rear chain converts the input force into a displacement at the trailing edge.

Finally, a confront between the microscopic and the macroscopic stress distribution can evidence the substantial differences at the base. Being the former the stress carried by the material at a molecular scale, it would reach the maximum in the empty elements next or directly linked to the Boundary Conditions. Its important to underline that with the term empty the author refers to elements characterized by a density = 0.1. Contrarily, the macroscopic stress follows the topology of the optimized structure. It will reach the tip in the elements directly linked to the application point of the input force.
